About the Project

The construction market in India is predicted to register unprecedented growth and become the worldâ€™s third largest by 2022 (Global Construction Perspectives and Oxford Economics, 2013). The construction industry, in India, is one of the largest consumers by volume of raw materials/ natural resources and construction materials/ products manufactured. India Energy Outlook: World Energy Outlook Special Report 2015 predicts that three quarters of the 2040 building stock is yet to be constructed in India. To fulfil this insatiable demand on energy and natural resources in a sustainable manner, it is imperative for us to leverage alternative construction materials and techniques.

The residential construction sector, in India, accounted for 270 billion units of electricity consumption in 2016, i.e. 23% of the total consumption. It is expected to double (580 billion units) in the next 15 years. Hence, recognizing the significance of energy efficient building materials and technologies, the Indo-German Energy Programme (IGEN) envisioned an Energy Conservation Building Code for Residential Buildings (ECBC-R) and was recently launched by the Ministry of Power, Government of India as the ECO Niwas Samhita 2018. The Code emphasizes on the role of green building design practices and construction material in making the residential buildings ecologically responsive, economically remunerative and occupant friendly. The implementation of this Code is expected to push for energy efficiency in residential sector. It will have potential for energy savings to the tune of 125 Billion Units of electricity by 2030, which is equivalent to about 100 million ton of CO2 emission.

The successful adoption of the ECBC-R would require compilation of a directory/ database for energy efficient building materials and a policy roadmap to guide formulation, enforcement and monitoring of labels, in the residential sector, through market forces.
